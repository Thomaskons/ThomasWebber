About Antony Goodman

Senior Case Officer | Cybercrime Investigation Unit

Expertise in Asset Recovery

Antony Goodman is a Senior Case Officer in HMRC's Asset Recovery Division, specializing in cybercrime. He leads asset seizure and recovery operations for online fraud, money laundering, and tax evasion cases—collaborating with UK and international agencies including the National Crime Agency (NCA).

His expertise encompasses digital forensics, blockchain tracing, and securing freezing orders. Antony mentors specialized teams and maintains close liaison with prosecutors to ensure successful case outcomes.

With a background in financial compliance and cyber enforcement, he is recognized for his technical-legal fusion approach in high-impact asset recovery operations.

Recovery Guidance

Clear steps to begin your asset recovery journey

1

Report the Crime

File an official report through the HMRC fraud reporting portal or Action Fraud. Early reporting increases recovery chances.

Report Now
2

Gather Evidence

Collect and preserve critical documentation:

  • Transaction IDs and receipts
  • Screenshots of communications
  • Detailed timelines of events
  • Account statements
  • Any correspondence with suspects
3

Immediate Actions

Protect yourself from further harm:

  • Secure all online accounts
  • Contact your bank to freeze assets
  • Document all suspicious activity
  • Avoid contact with suspects
  • Preserve digital evidence
4

What to Expect

Investigation Timeline: 6-18 months depending on complexity

Recovery Outcomes: Partial to full asset recovery possible based on case specifics

Your Role: Cooperation and timely evidence submission are crucial

Frequently Asked Questions

All information is handled in strict accordance with GDPR and UK data protection laws. Your data is accessed only by authorized personnel on a need-to-know basis. We implement secure communication channels and maintain confidentiality throughout the investigation process.

Investigation timelines vary based on case complexity. Simple cases may conclude within 6-9 months, while complex international cases involving cryptocurrency or multiple jurisdictions can take 12-18 months. We provide regular updates throughout the process.

Recovery outcomes depend on multiple factors including asset traceability, legal jurisdiction, and the stage of investigation. While we pursue maximum recovery, we cannot guarantee full compensation. Early reporting significantly improves recovery prospects.

Preserve all original digital evidence without alteration. Take screenshots, save emails and messages in their original format, maintain transaction records, and create detailed timelines. Do not attempt to investigate independently or confront suspects, as this may compromise the investigation.

Yes. HMRC collaborates with international agencies including Interpol, Europol, and foreign financial intelligence units. Cross-border cases require additional coordination and may extend investigation timelines, but we have established protocols for international asset recovery.

Our team specializes in blockchain tracing and cryptocurrency investigations. We utilize advanced forensic tools to track digital assets across multiple blockchains and exchanges. Cryptocurrency cases are complex but increasingly successful with proper evidence collection.

Privacy Policy
Data Collection and Usage

Information submitted through this website is processed in accordance with GDPR and UK data protection regulations. Personal data is used solely for the purpose of responding to inquiries and providing asset recovery guidance.

Data Retention

Submitted information and uploaded files are retained for 30 days or until case resolution, whichever is longer. Data is stored securely and accessed only by authorized personnel.
